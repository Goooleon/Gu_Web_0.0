Into Tibet
July - Aug. 2015

Tibet, the highest plateau on the planet earth, is a mystery and charming place attracting every human-beings. I travelled to Tibet by bike in the summer of 2015 to explore the place of mystery.

The composition of the image is rather simple but effective. there are only five organs and a graffiti-like dark background, showing the basic "sensors" of human beings. We interact with the outer world with them, and they are supposed to be a system in one, instead of being presented in a strange way and seperated. I did this in this way because what I want to show is that we are all just observers sometimes, or forever.

The idea of this illustration was derived from a nightmare that I had this afternoon. I had a really really really terrible dream that I ever had, which is not about horrible things like ghost or what but hollow in a dark. In that dream, there is no color, nothing exists but dark and my deep consciousness. I was there just watching everything or nothing in the dark, and felling everything but nothing. I was like I was falling in a blackhole but with no gravity tearing me up, things was in a peace, a definately peace. I felt that I was imprisoned in the dark hollow unlimited world, watching everything (nothing) passing by. I was trying to struggle, to fight, to yell, but I could do nothing but watch. At that moment, I was overwhelmed by the ever-lasting feeling of lonely, and It was so strong...
